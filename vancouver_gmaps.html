Free luggage storage

🌿 Granville Island Public Market
Take bus 50 from Davie St or walk down to the waterfront ferry dock. The market bursts with fresh seafood, artisan bread, local produce, and world-class doughnuts (Lee's Donuts — get there early!). Browse the galleries and craft studios in the surrounding arts district. This is Vancouver's creative soul.
Free entry · Budget $15–25 for food

🍞 Lunch at the Market
Grab lunch from the market stalls — Oyama Sausage Co., Siegel's Bagels, or the fresh oyster bar. Eat outside by the waterfront with views of False Creek and the Burrard Bridge.

🚲 Stanley Park Seawall Bike Ride
Ride the Aquabus from Granville Island to the Hornby St dock (~$4, 5 min) then walk up to Denman St to rent bikes. The 9 km seawall loop takes ~1.5–2 hours — ocean views, totem poles at Brockton Point, Siwash Rock, and the gorgeous Lions Gate Bridge backdrop. Stop at Prospect Point for the best panorama.
Aquabus ~$4/person · Bike rental ~$10–15/hr

Stroll from the hostel (~15 min walk or short bus) to Gastown. The famous Steam Clock at Cambie & Water St whistles every 15 minutes. Explore the cobblestone streets, independent boutiques, and the Victorian brick architecture. Great spot for dinner — try The Flying Pig for West Coast comfort food or Nuba for Lebanese.
Free to explore · Dinner ~$20–30/person

Day2
FlyOver Canada · Queen Elizabeth Park · Sunset Beach
May 31 · All transit · Stay: HI Vancouver Downtown Hostel
City Icons Park & Views FlyOver ~$40+ Sunset Free

📍 Day 2 · FlyOver Canada → Queen Elizabeth Park → Sunset Beach
🏠 HI Hostel → Walk / Bus 15 min → 🎢 Canada Place → Canada Line → King Edward → 🌸 Queen Elizabeth Park → Bus → Walk → 🌅 Sunset Beach
08:30
🥐 Hostel Breakfast
The hostel includes breakfast — make the most of it before heading out. A proper meal will power you through the morning.
Included in stay
09:30
🎢 FlyOver Canada — 4D Flight Experience
Located at 999 Canada Place, this is one of Vancouver's most unique attractions. You'll be suspended in a motion seat and "flown" across Canada's landscapes — Rockies, PEI coastline, Northern Lights — with wind, mist, and scent effects. The full experience takes ~45 min including pre-shows. Book online in advance — cheaper and skip the queue!
~$35–45/person (cheaper online)
11:00
🌊 Canada Place Waterfront Stroll
Walk the outer promenade of Canada Place — stunning views of the North Shore mountains, Burrard Inlet, and float planes landing on the water. Completely free. Great for photos.
Free
12:00
🍜 Lunch in Chinatown / Gastown Area
Walk 10 min east to Chinatown on Pender St — vibrant, affordable, and delicious. Try New Town Bakery for BBQ pork buns, or Phnom Penh for legendary crispy garlic chicken wings and butter beef. Budget-friendly and delicious.
~$12–18/person
14:00
🌸 Queen Elizabeth Park — City's Highest Point
Take Canada Line south to King Edward station, then Bus 15 up Cambie St (~5 min walk from bus stop). At 167 m, this park offers the best 360° panorama of Vancouver with the mountains as a backdrop. The Bloedel Conservatory is a tropical dome full of free-flying parrots and exotic birds — worth the $7 entry. The sunken gardens are spectacular in late May/June.
Park free · Conservatory ~$7/person
17:00
🌅 Sunset Beach — Golden Hour
Take Canada Line back north, walk from Yaletown-Roundhouse to Sunset Beach (10 min). In late May, sunset is around 9 PM — so enjoy the beach in the long golden afternoon light. Watch the English Bay scene come alive with kayakers, cyclists, and sunset chasers. Grab ice cream from English Bay Café and sit on the sand.
Free
Evening
🍷 Dinner on Davie St or Denman St
Both streets are steps from the hostel and packed with great options — from sushi and Thai to wood-fired pizza and Mexican. Great neighbourhood energy on a Saturday night.
📌 Day 2 Tips: FlyOver Canada is best booked online (save ~$10 and choose your slot). Queen Elizabeth Park is reachable in 20 min from downtown — don't skip it, the view is genuinely stunning. Since sunset in Vancouver on May 31 is around 9:05 PM, you have a very long, beautiful evening to enjoy.

Bowen Island All Day · Check Out + Move to Richmond

📍 Day 3 · Horseshoe Bay Ferry Terminal → Bowen Island
🏠 HI Hostel → Canada Line → Bus 257 ~70 min → ⛴ Horseshoe Bay → BC Ferries ~20 min → 🏝 Bowen Island Snug Cove
07:30
🧳 Hostel Breakfast + Check Out
Enjoy hostel breakfast, then check out and store your bigger bags at left luggage (or take everything — the Airbnb check-in is flexible). Grab your day bag and head out early to catch a morning ferry.
08:15
🚌 Transit to Horseshoe Bay
From City Centre Canada Line station, take Canada Line south one stop to Waterfront, then walk up to Bus 257 (Horseshoe Bay Express — direct, no transfers). About 70 min total. Scenic ride through West Vancouver. Arrive early at the terminal — BC Ferries to Bowen Island stops selling tickets 10 min before sailing.
~$3.25/person (Compass Card)
09:45
⛴ BC Ferries: Horseshoe Bay → Snug Cove
Tickets are walk-up only — no reservations. A 20-minute crossing across glassy Howe Sound, framed by snowcapped Coast Mountains. Return journey is included in your ticket. Go up top for the best views and the sea air.
~$11–13/person (return included)
10:15
☕ Snug Cove Village — Morning Wander
Step off the ferry into Snug Cove — a little village of galleries, boutiques, and cafes. Try The Snug Café for Eggs Benedict or Artisan Eats for fresh pastries. Browse the artisan shops and soak in the slow island pace.
11:30
🥾 Crippen Regional Park Hike
Start from Snug Cove and enter the free Crippen Park trail network. The Killarney Creek Loop (~3.5 km, 1.5 hrs) winds through old-growth forest past a small lake and a salmon-bearing creek. Or take the shorter Pebbly Beach trail (30 min) for ocean views without the elevation.
Free
13:30
🏖 Deep Bay / Sandy Beach Relaxation
10 min walk from Snug Cove, a quiet pebble-and-sand beach looks out over Howe Sound. Perfect for sitting, paddling, and mountain-gazing. Total serenity.
Free
Afternoon
🚣 Sea Kayaking (Optional)
Kayak rentals are right near the ferry dock. Paddling the calm waters of Howe Sound with mountain walls rising on all sides is genuinely magical. Self-guided 2-hr rental or guided 3-hr tour available. Beginner-friendly.
Self-rental ~$40/2 hrs · Guided tour ~$85/person
17:00
🍦 Branch on Bowen Ice Cream + Return Ferry
Get a scoop from Branch on Bowen (home of Earnest Ice Cream) while waiting for the 5–6 PM ferry back to Horseshoe Bay. Check the BC Ferries schedule before heading in. The return crossing is included in your ticket.
19:30
🏠 Check In to Richmond Airbnb
Take Bus 257 back to downtown, Canada Line to Brighouse, then Bus 401 south to No.1 Rd. Coordinate check-in time with your host. Pick up luggage from storage if you left it downtown. Settle in — this is your home base for the next 5 nights!
~$3.25/person transit
📌 Day 3 Tips: Bowen Island ferries cannot be reserved — arrive at Horseshoe Bay ~30 min early on busy weekends. You do not need to bring your car (saves ~$45 in vehicle fare!) — Snug Cove and Crippen Park are completely walkable from the dock. The return ferry is free (included in the outbound ticket). If you want to carry less on this day, coordinate a mid-afternoon Airbnb check-in with your host.
Day4
Richmond Food Day · Dim Sum + Steveston Wharf
Jun 2 · No car needed · Bus + Canada Line · Stay: Richmond Airbnb

📍 Day 4 · 10060 Albion Rd → Aberdeen Centre → Steveston
🏠 Albion Rd → Bus 401 → Brighouse → Canada Line → 🏬 Aberdeen Centre → Bus to Steveston → 🦐 Steveston Wharf → Walk 10 min → 🌅 Garry Point Park
Morning
🍱 Alexandra Road — Richmond's Golden Mile
Known as the "Golden Mile," Alexandra Rd (between No.1 Rd and No.5 Rd) is one of the best Asian restaurant strips in North America. From HK-style BBQ and Shanghainese xiaolongbao to Vietnamese pho and Taiwanese scallion pancakes — breakfast and early lunch options are everywhere. Get there before noon for the best dim sum selection.
Breakfast/Lunch ~$10–18/person
10:30
🏬 Aberdeen Centre + Lansdowne Centre
Canada Line to Aberdeen station — the mall is directly connected to the platform. Browse the 3rd-floor food court (Taiwanese milk tea, Japanese curry, HK egg waffles), Japanese variety shops, and bubble tea brands. Then walk to adjacent Lansdowne Centre for mainstream shopping. CF Richmond Centre (largest mainstream mall in Richmond) is one stop north at Brighouse.
Free to browse
12:00
🍤 Dim Sum at a Top Cantonese Restaurant
Richmond has arguably the best Cantonese dim sum outside Hong Kong. Top picks near Aberdeen: Chef Tony Seafood Restaurant (creative dim sum, book ahead), Sea Harbour Seafood, or Fisherman's Terrace. Lunch service is much more affordable than dinner.
~$25–40/person (book in advance!)
14:00
🦐 Steveston Fisherman's Wharf — Live Spot Prawns
Take Bus 401 south from No.1 Rd toward No.5 Rd, then Bus 401 south direction or a short taxi to Steveston (~25–30 min total). Historic Steveston Village is a beautifully preserved cannery town. At the wharf, fishing boats sell live spot prawns, Dungeness crab, and sea urchin directly to the public. June is peak spot prawn season — get them steamed right on the dock. Stroll the wooden boardwalk and explore the Gulf of Georgia Cannery museum.
Spot prawns ~$25–30/lb · Free to explore village
16:30
🌅 Garry Point Park — Steveston Sunset Stroll
A 10-min walk from the Wharf, Garry Point Park sits at the tip of the Steveston Peninsula with 180° views over the Strait of Georgia. Japanese gardens, open grass, and stunning evening light — perfect for a slow end to a full day. In June sunset is after 9 PM, so there's no rush heading home.
Free
Evening
🧋 Richmond Bubble Tea Night
Head back to central Richmond via bus. No.3 Rd after dark is lined with neon bubble tea and dessert shops — Coco, Gong Cha, Tiger Sugar, Meet Fresh. A relaxed low-key wind-down before the big Sea to Sky day tomorrow.
~$6–8/drink
📌 Day 4 Tips: Steveston is reachable without a car — take a cab from Aberdeen (~$15) or Bus 407/401 toward No.5 Rd. Spot prawn timing matters: boats usually start selling around noon, but if you arrive at 2–3 PM, selection may be limited — aim to get there by 2:30 PM at the latest. The Richmond Night Market (Fri/Sat/Sun only) is saved for Day 7, which lands on a Friday — perfect timing!

🚌 Head to Waterfront via Canada Line
Bus 401 → Brighouse → Canada Line to Waterfront (~40 min from Richmond). Walk to the SeaBus terminal (connected to Waterfront station). Grab a coffee at one of the Waterfront shops before boarding.
$3.25/person (Compass Card)

⛴ SeaBus to Lonsdale Quay
12-minute ferry crossing of Burrard Inlet with outstanding views of Vancouver's downtown skyline and the mountains behind. Included in your regular Compass Card fare — no extra charge. Browse Lonsdale Quay market briefly (it opens at 9 AM) before catching Bus 228.
09:30
🌲 Lynn Canyon Suspension Bridge — FREE!
Bus 228 (Lynn Valley direction) from Lonsdale Quay to Peters Rd (~20 min), then a 5-min walk into Lynn Canyon Park. The free suspension bridge hangs 50 m above the canyon gorge — just as dramatic as Capilano, without the $60 admission fee. After crossing, take the Twin Falls Loop trail (1.5 km, 25 min) to see the twin waterfalls and the stunning 30 Foot Pool (an emerald natural swimming hole — safe in summer).
Completely FREE
11:00
🌊 Cleveland Dam (Optional Extension)
If energy allows, take a short taxi or Uber to Cleveland Dam (~10 min from Lynn Canyon, no easy bus connection). The dam retains Capilano Lake — the short walk along the top offers gorgeous views of the Capilano Canyon below and Grouse Mountain above. The Dam to Bridge Trail (~1.5 hr hike) winds through old-growth forest. Parking: 5077 Dam Rd.
Free · Taxi ~$12 one-way
12:30
🍩 Lunch + Honey's Donuts at Deep Cove
Bus 212 from Phibbs Exchange to Deep Cove (check TransLink for transfer from Lynn Canyon). Deep Cove is a charming waterfront village at the head of Indian Arm fjord. Honey's Donuts is legendary — the lineup is long but moves fast. Get a maple-glazed and eat it by the dock with a view of the still water and forested mountains.
Donuts ~$2–3 each · Lunch ~$15
14:00
🚣 Deep Cove Kayaking
Deep Cove Canoe & Kayak rents double kayaks right at the waterfront. Paddle out into Indian Arm — a 70 km fjord flanked by mountains. The water is calm and sheltered, perfect for beginners. A 2-hour paddle takes you past rocky shores and forested slopes. Absolutely unforgettable.
Double kayak ~$30–40/2 hrs per boat
Evening
🍜 Return to Richmond for Dinner
Bus 212 → Phibbs Exchange → Bus 210 to downtown → Canada Line back to Richmond. Grab a bowl of hot noodles from the Alexandra Rd strip — you've earned it after all that paddling.

📌 Day 5 Tips — Car or No Car? If you want to do the full combo (Lynn Canyon + Cleveland Dam 1.5-hr hike + Deep Cove kayaking), renting a car for Day 5 is worth it. There's no direct bus between Lynn Canyon and Cleveland Dam — you'd need a taxi (~$12–15 one way), then another taxi back to main transit for Deep Cove, which adds ~45 min of friction and ~$30 in taxi fares. With a car, the three spots form a clean 30-min loop on the North Shore. If you drop Cleveland Dam and just do Lynn Canyon + Deep Cove, transit by SeaBus + Bus 228 + Bus 212 works perfectly and costs ~$6.50 total. Your call — the car saves time; transit saves money. Lynn Canyon bridge remains 100% free either way. Deep Cove kayak rentals: arrive between 1–3 PM on weekdays for shortest wait. Bring water shoes.

📍 Day 6 · Richmond → Cypress → Shannon Falls → Sea to Sky Gondola

07:30
🚗 Pick Up Rental Car at Brighouse
Enterprise or Budget near Brighouse Canada Line station — book in advance online to get the best rate and guarantee availability. Check out early for the best driving conditions on the Sea to Sky. This is your only car rental day of the whole trip.
~$60–80/day incl. tax (book online)
08:30
🔭 Cypress Mountain Lookout — Panoramic Vancouver View
Drive north over the Lions Gate Bridge and up Cypress Bowl Rd to the Cypress Mountain Lookout. On a clear day, the view sweeps over Vancouver's entire cityscape, the Fraser Delta, and Mount Baker in Washington State. The 2010 Olympic venue is up here too. Free to visit, just park in the lot. Great morning light for photos.
Free · Parking free
09:30
🌊 Whytecliff Park — Hidden Ocean Gem
A short detour from Cypress on Marine Dr brings you to Whytecliff Park — a rocky marine park with stunning ocean-carved bluffs and tide pools. On a clear day you can see Bowen Island just across the water (you were there two days ago!). Perfect photo spot. Free, limited parking.
Free
10:30
💦 Shannon Falls Provincial Park
Continue north on the Sea to Sky Highway (Hwy 99) to Squamish. Shannon Falls is BC's third-highest waterfall at 335 m — and it's just a 5-minute walk from the free parking lot. The roar is audible from the car park. Bring a light jacket as it creates its own microclimate.
Free (parking ~$5)
11:30
⛰ Sea to Sky Gondola — Summit Views
Just 2 min drive from Shannon Falls. The gondola lifts you 885 m up to the Summit Lodge in under 10 minutes — views of Howe Sound, glaciers, and Squamish Chief granite monolith. At the top: a sky pilot trail, suspension bridge walk, and the Summit Lodge café with huge windows. Allow 2–3 hours up top.
$73.95/person · Parking ~$10/day
15:00
🚗 Drive Back to Richmond
Head back south along the Sea to Sky Highway — the return drive is just as beautiful. Stop at a Squamish roadside diner for a late lunch if you haven't eaten. Back in Richmond by mid-afternoon.
17:00
🚗 Return Car + Relax
Return the rental car to Brighouse. Head back to the Airbnb and unwind — it's been a big day. Grab a simple dinner from a nearby noodle shop on Alexandra Rd or order delivery. Rest up for tomorrow's UBC and Night Market day!
📌 Day 6 Tips: This is your only driving day — make it count! Check that the Gondola is operating before leaving (weather closures occasionally happen) and book tickets online in advance. The Sea to Sky Highway is stunning in both directions, so enjoy the drive. Return the car by early evening to keep costs down — and save your appetite, because tomorrow is the Richmond Night Market!

10:00
🏛 Museum of Anthropology (MOA)
One of Canada's finest museums — and one of Vancouver's most underrated attractions. The iconic Arthur Erickson building houses an extraordinary collection of First Nations art: towering totem poles inside a glass Great Hall, Haida longhouses in the outdoor garden, and the world-renowned Raven and the First Men sculpture by Bill Reid. Allow 1.5–2 hrs. The museum sits on a bluff with ocean views towards the North Shore mountains.
~$18/person (Tuesdays free after 5 PM)
12:00
🌲 Pacific Spirit Regional Park Trail
Right behind UBC, Pacific Spirit Park has 73 km of free hiking trails through old-growth forest — Douglas fir, western red cedar, and towering hemlock. Pick up the Sword Fern Trail or Salal Trail (both easy, ~45 min loops). The forest here is genuinely lush, mossy, and serene. A beautiful contrast to the museum.
Free
13:30
🏖 Spanish Banks Beach
Walk 20 min north from the park to Spanish Banks — one of Vancouver's best and most expansive beaches. At low tide the sand flats stretch endlessly, and the views across English Bay to the North Shore mountains are spectacular. In June the water is cold but the beach is fantastic for a picnic, a paddle, or just lying in the sun. Bring food from MOA café or pick up a sandwich at the UBC bus loop before heading down.
Free
15:30
🌊 Walk the Foreshore to Locarno Beach (Optional)
The shoreline from Spanish Banks connects to Locarno Beach and then Jericho Beach — you can walk the entire foreshore for about 3 km with uninterrupted water views. The afternoon light on the mountains is exceptional. A perfect wind-down after the museum.
Free
17:30
🍕 Dinner in Kitsilano — W 4th Ave
Bus 4 east from Jericho Beach (or Bus 9 from UBC) drops you on W 4th Ave — Kitsilano's vibrant restaurant strip. Options range from casual to upscale: Nook for wood-fired pizza, Fable for farm-to-table, or grab fish tacos from the many takeout spots along the strip. Afterwards, walk down to Kitsilano Beach for the last golden hour of the day.
Dinner ~$18–30/person

🍕 Early Dinner in Kitsilano — W 4th Ave
Bus 4 east from Jericho Beach (or Bus 9 from UBC) drops you on W 4th Ave — Kitsilano's vibrant restaurant strip. Eat early so you have energy for the night market. Try Nook for wood-fired pizza, Fable for farm-to-table, or grab fish tacos from a takeout spot along the strip.
